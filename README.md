## What's here

A few things I'm working on, in case any of it is useful to you too.

**[awesome-ai-pm](https://github.com/isumitsoni/awesome-ai-pm)**
A curated list of resources for PMs navigating AI - tools, evals, learning resources, and things worth knowing. Updated as I find things worth sharing.

**[pm-prompts](https://github.com/isumitsoni/pm-prompts)**
A library of prompts I use in my actual PM workflow - discovery, strategy, stakeholder communication, AI feature specs. Nothing theoretical. Just things that work. 48 prompts across 8 categories.

**[ai-feature-specs](https://github.com/isumitsoni/ai-feature-specs)**
Production-ready spec templates for AI features. Covers RAG, summarization, classification, content generation, conversation agents, and recommendations. Each template has the cost/latency budget and safety controls built in.

**[vibe-coding-playbook](https://github.com/isumitsoni/vibe-coding-playbook)**
5 playbooks for PMs and founders who build with AI coding tools. Covers the full arc from discovery to production.

**[claude-code-for-founders](https://github.com/isumitsoni/claude-code-for-founders)**
A practical course for non-technical founders shipping AI products. 5 modules covering build OS setup, MVP scoping, evals, production hardening, and unit economics.

**[ai-pm-interview-kit](https://github.com/isumitsoni/ai-pm-interview-kit)**
Interview prep for AI PM roles. Practice prompts and scoring rubrics for the 5 question types showing up in 2026 hiring loops: behavioral, product design, prioritization, tradeoffs, and metrics.

**[purepaste](https://github.com/isumitsoni/purepaste)**
A small side project. I kept getting annoyed by how robotic AI-generated text sounds, so I built a simple tool to clean it up. Live at [purepaste.pro](https://purepaste.pro). Built with Claude API and Next.js in a weekend.

## About me

8+ years as a PM across B2C and B2B products. I spent 7 years in travel tech, the last few as Founding PM at Travel Plus where I owned the product end to end.

In parallel, I ran a startup called Product Academy for a few years. It started as a way to help companies hire faster and grew into a team of 15 people across hiring and upskilling programs. We shut it down after COVID hit and the hiring market dried up. That whole experience - building a team, figuring out pricing, watching it fall apart - changed how I think about product work in ways that being a PM at a company never quite did.

Currently working as a Senior PM at a US-based company, remotely from India. I work closely with engineers and have a lot of respect for the craft. Lately I've also been trying to build things myself, using AI tools, just to understand what's actually hard and what isn't. I'm writing about what I learn on [LinkedIn](https://linkedin.com/in/isumitsoni) if you want to follow along.
